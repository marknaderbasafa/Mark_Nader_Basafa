Historian and Philosopher of Science

Mark Nader Basafa

I work on Thomas S. Kuhn, twentieth-century philosophy of science, scientific historicism, and the intellectual formation of The Structure of Scientific Revolutions. My research reconstructs Kuhn’s pre-Structure development through archival evidence, with special attention to Harvard’s philosophical milieu, critical realism, historicism, Cassirer, Fleck, Lovejoy, Koyré, Sarton, and Conant.

About

I am a researcher in the history and philosophy of science specializing in Thomas Kuhn, scientific rationality, historicism, and the epistemology of AI-mediated knowledge. My work combines archival research and systematic philosophy to reconstruct scientific rationality as historically generated, collectively stabilized, symbolically mediated, and professionally transmitted. My current research extends this framework to artificial intelligence through the concepts of digital thought collectives, co-thinking, and constrained rational normativity.

Research

Philosophy of artificial intelligence; history and philosophy of science; AI-mediated scientific knowledge; scientific rationality and progress; Thomas Kuhn; digital thought collectives; collective cognition; epistemic systems; historicism; Cassirer, Fleck, Mannheim, Koyré, and Lovejoy.

Kuhn’s Harvard

A study of Kuhn’s Harvard formation, including Sarton, Conant, Lovejoy, Russell, Langer, Frank, Koyré, and the tensions between logical and historicist approaches to science.

Cassirer, Fleck, and Historicist Rationality

Archival work on Kuhn’s marginalia and early lectures, focusing on symbolic forms, thought collectives, textbook science, and historically situated normativity.

Scientific Change

Work on paradigm shifts, revolutions, incommensurability, theory-ladenness, and the possibility of rational constraint without ahistorical foundations.
